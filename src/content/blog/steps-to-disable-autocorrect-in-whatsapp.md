Have you ever been frustrated by your Android phone automatically **correcting** your messages in WhatsApp, often to hilarious or embarrassing results? Autocorrect can be helpful for fixing typos, but sometimes it changes words you didn’t intend. Luckily, it’s easy to turn off the autocorrect feature in WhatsApp on Android devices. In this article, we’ll walk through the simple steps to disable autocorrect so you can type freely without your phone trying to outsmart you.

### Why Turn Off Autocorrect?

Before we dive into the steps, let’s discuss some reasons you might want to disable the autocorrect feature in WhatsApp:

- **Avoid embarrassing mistakes**: Autocorrect fails can lead to some awkward situations if your phone changes a word to something completely different than you intended. Disabling autocorrect ensures your messages say exactly what you want.

- **Use slang, abbreviations, or non-standard language**: If you frequently use slang, abbreviations, or words not found in the standard dictionary, autocorrect will likely try to change them. Turning it off lets you use your preferred lingo without interference.

- **Type in multiple languages**: For multilingual users who message in different languages, autocorrect based on one language’s dictionary can mangle words in the other language. Disabling autocorrect makes it easier to switch between languages.

- **Have more control**: Some people simply prefer to have full control over their typing and don’t want their phone making automated changes, even if some typos slip through.

Whatever your reason, disabling autocorrect in WhatsApp on Android is a straightforward process. However, it’s important to note that the autocorrect setting is controlled by your phone’s keyboard, not the WhatsApp app itself. This means the exact steps may vary slightly depending on your Android device and keyboard app.

### Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to turn off autocorrect for WhatsApp on most Android devices:

**Step 1: Open your Android settings**

From your phone’s home screen or app drawer, tap the **Settings** icon to open the device settings.

**Step 2: Find the keyboard settings**

The location of keyboard settings varies by Android version and manufacturer. Look for one of the following options:

- **System > Languages & input > Virtual keyboard**

- **General management > Language and input > On-screen keyboard**

- In the Settings search bar, type **keyboard** and select the relevant result

**Step 3: Select your keyboard**

On the keyboard settings page, you’ll see a list of all the virtual keyboards installed on your device. Tap the name of the keyboard you use for typing in WhatsApp. Common options include:

- Gboard (Google Keyboard)

- Samsung Keyboard

- SwiftKey Keyboard

**Step 4: Find the text correction settings**

Once you’ve selected your keyboard, look for an option related to text correction, such as:

- **Text correction**

- **Smart typing**

- **Predictive text**

Tap this option to access the text correction settings for your keyboard.

**Step 5: Turn off autocorrect**

On the text correction settings page, find the autocorrect option. This may be labeled as:

- **Auto-correction**

- **Auto replace**

- **Autocorrect**

Toggle the switch next to this option to the off position. The exact wording and layout may differ depending on your keyboard, but the process is generally the same.

**Step 6: Test it out**

Open WhatsApp and start typing a message to confirm autocorrect is disabled. Try typing some misspelled words or slang terms – they should appear exactly as you typed them, without any automatic corrections.

That’s it! You’ve now successfully turned off autocorrect for WhatsApp on your Android device. If you ever want to re-enable autocorrect, simply follow the same steps and toggle the autocorrect option back on.

### Tips and Troubleshooting

Here are a few additional tips and common issues to keep in mind:

- **Autocorrect vs. predictive text**: Some keyboards have separate options for autocorrect (automatically changing misspelled words) and predictive text (suggesting words as you type). If you only want to disable autocorrect but keep predictive suggestions, make sure to toggle off the right setting.

- **Can’t find the autocorrect setting**: If you don’t see an autocorrect option in your keyboard settings, try looking for other text correction settings like **Auto replace** or **Predictive text** – these may control the autocorrect functionality on some keyboards.

- **Using a different keyboard app**: If you use a third-party keyboard app like SwiftKey or Grammarly Keyboard, the steps to disable autocorrect may differ slightly. Check the app’s settings or help documentation for specific instructions.

- **Autocorrect still happening**: If you’ve turned off autocorrect but your phone is still making automatic corrections, try restarting your device or double-check that you disabled the right setting. In rare cases, you may need to update your keyboard app or try a different keyboard.

### Conclusion

Autocorrect can be a double-edged sword – helpful when it catches typos, but frustrating when it changes your intended words. By following the simple steps outlined in this article, you can easily disable autocorrect for WhatsApp on your Android device and take back control of your typing.

Whether you want to avoid embarrassing mistakes, use non-standard language, or simply prefer unfiltered typing, turning off autocorrect is a quick customization that can make your WhatsApp experience smoother. Just remember that the setting is controlled by your keyboard rather than the WhatsApp app itself.

While disabling autocorrect means you’ll need to be a bit more careful with your typing, the freedom to express yourself exactly as you intend is well worth the tradeoff for many users. Plus, you can always re-enable autocorrect later if you change your mind.

Happy typing!
