Apple Music is one of the most popular music streaming services, with over 75 million songs available to stream or download. While it’s made by Apple, you don’t need an iPhone or other Apple device to enjoy it. Apple Music is also available on Android phones and tablets, letting you access all your favorite music no matter what device you use.

In this article, we’ll walk through the steps to get Apple Music up and running on your Android phone. It’s a quick and easy process that will have you streaming your favorite tunes in no time.

### Step 1: Download the Apple Music App

The first step is to download the official Apple Music app on your Android device. Open the Google Play Store app, then search for **Apple Music**. Look for the app published by Apple Inc. and tap the Install button. The app is free to download.

Make sure your Android phone is running version 5.0 (Lollipop) or later. You can check your Android version by going to Settings > System > About phone.

### Step 2: Open the App and Start Your Free Trial

Once the app finishes installing, open it from your app drawer or home screen. The first time you launch Apple Music, you’ll see a welcome screen offering a free trial. Apple lets new subscribers try the full Apple Music experience for 3 months at no charge.

Tap the trial offer, then select the subscription plan you want: Individual, Student, or Family. Individual plans cost $9.99/month after the trial ends. Student plans are discounted to $4.99/month with a verified college enrollment. Family plans are $14.99/month and can be shared with up to 6 people.

### Step 3: Sign In With Your Apple ID

After selecting a plan, you’ll be prompted to sign in with an Apple ID. If you already have one, tap Use Existing Apple ID and enter your credentials. If you don’t have an Apple ID, tap Create New Apple ID and follow the steps to make one. You’ll need to provide a valid email address, create a password, and enter a payment method for after the trial.

Once you’re signed in, you may be asked to agree to the terms and conditions. Review them and tap Agree.

### Step 4: Start Listening to Music!

Congratulations, you’re now subscribed to Apple Music! The app will guide you through some initial setup, like selecting your favorite music genres and artists. This helps Apple Music learn your tastes and make better recommendations.

The app has five tabs at the bottom:

- **Listen Now:** A personalized feed of album and playlist recommendations, plus your recently played music.

- **Browse:** Explore new releases, exclusive content, curated playlists, videos, and more.

- **Radio:** Listen to Apple Music’s live radio stations, including Apple Music 1, or check out curated genre stations.

- **Library:** View your full music library, including songs you’ve added from Apple Music or uploaded from your own collection.

- **Search:** Look up any artist, album, song, playlist, or video in the Apple Music catalog.

Tap any tab to start discovering music. When you find a song or album you like, tap the Play button to start streaming. You can also add it to your Library, download it for offline listening, or add it to a playlist.

### Step 5: Optional – Use Apple Music With Other Apps

In addition to the main app, there are a couple other ways to enjoy Apple Music on Android:

- **Android Auto:** If your car supports Android Auto, you can control Apple Music playback and browse your library from your vehicle’s in-dash display.

- **Chromecast:** Apple Music supports Google Chromecast, so you can cast music to compatible speakers or TVs.

To use either of these features, make sure your phone is on the same Wi-Fi network as your Android Auto or Chromecast device. Then open Apple Music and tap the cast icon to connect.

## Conclusion

Apple Music is a fantastic music streaming service, and now you know how to use it on your Android phone. Whether you’re a new subscriber ready to start your free trial, or an existing member who just switched to Android, it only takes a few taps to get up and running.

With Apple Music on Android, you’ll have access to over 75 million songs, curated radio stations and playlists, personalized recommendations, and more. Plus, you can listen across your other devices, including smart speakers, cars, computers, and more.

So what are you waiting for? Download the Apple Music app on your Android phone and start streaming your favorite music today!
