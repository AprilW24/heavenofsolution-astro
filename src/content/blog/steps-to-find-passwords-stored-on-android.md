Remembering multiple passwords for different accounts can be a daunting task. That’s where the password manager on your Android phone comes in handy. It securely stores your login credentials, allowing you to access your accounts with just a few taps. However, finding and managing these saved passwords can be confusing if you’re not familiar with the process.

By following the steps outlined in this article, you’ll learn how to locate, view, edit, and even export your saved passwords on your Android device. Whether you need to access a specific account or want to review your password security, this guide will provide you with the necessary information.

## Steps to Find Passwords on Your Android Phone

### Step 1: Open Google Chrome

The password manager on your Android phone is integrated with the Google Chrome browser. To access your saved passwords, you’ll need to open the Chrome app.

- Locate and tap the Chrome app icon on your Android device.

- Once the app is open, tap on the three-dot menu icon in the top-right corner.

### Step 2: Access the Password Manager

After opening the Chrome menu, follow these steps to access the Password Manager:

- Tap on **Settings** from the menu options.

- Scroll down and tap on **Passwords**.

- You may be prompted to enter your device’s screen lock credentials (PIN, pattern, or password) or your Google account password for security purposes.

### Step 3: View and Manage Saved Passwords

Once you’ve accessed the Password Manager, you’ll see a list of all the websites and apps for which you’ve saved passwords. Here’s what you can do:

- **View Passwords**: Tap on any website or app entry to view the saved username and password. You’ll need to confirm your screen lock credentials or Google account password to reveal the password.

- **Edit Passwords**: If you need to update a saved password, tap on the entry, and then tap the **Edit** button. You can modify the username, password, or both, and then tap **Save** to update the entry.

- **Delete Passwords**: To remove a saved password, tap on the entry, and then tap the trash can icon in the top-right corner. Confirm the deletion when prompted.

- **Export Passwords**: If you need to export your saved passwords (e.g., for backup purposes), tap the three-dot menu icon in the top-right corner of the Password Manager screen, and then select **Export passwords**. You’ll be prompted to enter your device’s screen lock credentials or Google account password before the passwords are exported.

**Note:** It’s important to keep your screen lock credentials and Google account password secure, as they provide access to your saved passwords.

### Step 4: Check for Compromised Passwords (Optional)

Google’s Password Manager also includes a feature called **Password Checkup** that can help you identify if any of your saved passwords have been compromised in a data breach. Here’s how to use it:

- In the Password Manager screen, tap on the **Check passwords** button.

- Google will analyze your saved passwords and inform you if any of them have been compromised or are considered weak.

- If any compromised passwords are found, you’ll be prompted to update them immediately for better security.

## Conclusion

Managing your passwords on your Android phone is a crucial step in maintaining your online security. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can easily find, view, edit, and export your saved passwords using the built-in password manager. Additionally, the Password Checkup feature helps you identify and update any compromised or weak passwords, ensuring that your accounts remain secure.

Remember, while the password manager on your Android phone is a convenient tool, it’s essential to use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication whenever possible. By taking these precautions, you can significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access to your sensitive information.

Stay safe and secure in the digital world by taking advantage of the password management features on your Android device!
