Have you ever accidentally deleted an important video from your Android phone and panicked, thinking it was lost forever? Losing precious memories captured in videos can be heartbreaking. But don’t worry! There are several ways to recover deleted videos from your Android device without needing a computer. In this article, we’ll guide you through the steps to retrieve your lost videos for free.

## 1. Check the Recently Deleted Folder

The first place to look for your deleted videos is the **Recently Deleted** or **Trash** folder in your Android phone’s Gallery app. When you delete a video, it is moved to this folder and kept for 30 days before being permanently deleted. Here’s how to restore videos from the Recently Deleted folder:

- Open the **Gallery** app on your Android phone.

- Go to the **Albums** tab and scroll down to find the **Recently Deleted** or **Trash** folder.

- Tap on the folder to view the deleted videos.

- Select the videos you want to recover and tap on the **Restore** button.

Your selected videos will be restored to their original location in the Gallery app.

## 2. Recover Videos from Google Photos

If you have backed up your videos to **Google Photos** before deleting them, you can easily recover them from there. Google Photos keeps deleted items in the trash for 60 days. Follow these steps to recover videos from Google Photos:

- Open the **Google Photos** app on your Android device.

- Tap on the **Library** tab at the bottom.

- Select the **Trash** folder.

- Find and select the videos you want to recover.

- Tap on the **Restore** button to get your videos back.

The restored videos will reappear in your Google Photos library and Gallery app.

## 3. Use Google Drive to Retrieve Deleted Videos

Another way to recover deleted videos is by using **Google Drive**, provided you have synced your videos to Drive before losing them. Here’s how to retrieve your videos from Google Drive:

- Open the **Google Drive** app on your Android phone.

- Tap on the **Menu** icon (three horizontal lines) and select **Trash**.

- Locate the deleted videos you want to recover.

- Select the videos and tap on the **Restore** button.

The videos will be restored to their original location in Google Drive and your phone’s Gallery app.

## 4. Try Third-Party Data Recovery Apps

If the above methods don’t work, you can try using third-party data recovery apps available on the Google Play Store. These apps scan your device’s storage and attempt to recover deleted files, including videos. Some popular data recovery apps include:

- **DiskDigger Photo Recovery**

- **EaseUS MobiSaver**

- **Dumpster**

- **GT Recovery**

However, keep in mind that the success of these apps depends on factors like the time elapsed since deletion and whether the deleted data has been overwritten. It’s best to use these apps as a last resort and stop using your phone immediately after realizing you have deleted important videos to increase the chances of recovery.

## Conclusion

Losing important videos on your Android phone can be stressful, but there are several ways to recover them without using a computer. By checking the Recently Deleted folder, utilizing cloud services like Google Photos and Google Drive, or trying third-party data recovery apps, you have a good chance of getting your precious videos back.

To avoid losing videos in the future, make it a habit to regularly back up your Android phone’s data to cloud storage or your computer. This way, even if you accidentally delete videos or encounter other data loss scenarios, you can easily restore them from your backups.

Remember, the sooner you attempt to recover deleted videos, the higher the chances of success. Act quickly, and you’ll be able to relive those special moments captured in your videos once again.
