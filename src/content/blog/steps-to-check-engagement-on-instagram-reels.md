Instagram Reels have become an essential part of any social media strategy, offering brands and creators a powerful way to reach new audiences and boost engagement. But how do you know if your Reels are actually performing well? In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to check engagement on Instagram Reels, helping you understand your content’s performance and refine your strategy for better results.

## Introduction

Since its launch in 2020, Instagram Reels has quickly become one of the platform’s most popular features. These short-form videos offer a creative way to showcase your brand, products, or personality, and they’ve proven to be highly effective at driving engagement and reach. However, to truly harness the power of Reels, you need to understand how they’re performing and what metrics matter most.

Checking engagement on Instagram Reels isn’t just about vanity metrics – it’s about gaining valuable insights that can inform your content strategy, help you understand your audience better, and ultimately drive real results for your business or personal brand. Whether you’re a seasoned social media pro or just getting started with Reels, this guide will equip you with the knowledge and tools you need to measure and improve your Reels performance.

## Steps to Check Engagement on Instagram Reels

### 1. Ensure You Have a Business or Creator Account

Before you can access detailed insights for your Reels, you need to make sure you have the right type of Instagram account. Personal accounts don’t have access to in-depth analytics, so you’ll need to switch to either a Business or Creator account.

**To switch to a Business or Creator account:**

- Go to your profile and tap the menu icon in the top right corner

- Select “Settings”

- Tap “Account”

- Scroll down and select “Switch to Professional Account”

- Choose either “Business” or “Creator” based on your needs

Once you’ve made the switch, you’ll have access to Instagram Insights, including detailed metrics for your Reels.

### 2. Navigate to Your Reels Insights

Now that you have the right account type, it’s time to dive into your Reels insights. There are a few ways to access this information:

**Method 1: From your profile**

- Go to your profile page

- Tap the Insights button below your bio

- Scroll down to the “Content You Shared” section

- Tap on “Reels”

**Method 2: From an individual Reel**

- Open one of your Reels

- Tap the three-dot menu in the bottom right corner

- Select “Insights”

Either method will give you access to a wealth of information about your Reels performance.

### 3. Understand Key Engagement Metrics

Once you’re in the Reels Insights section, you’ll see a variety of metrics. Here are some of the most important ones to focus on:

- **Plays**: The number of times your Reel has been played. This is a good indicator of how many people are actually watching your content.

- **Likes**: The number of likes your Reel has received. This shows how many people enjoyed your content enough to take action.

- **Comments**: The number of comments on your Reel. High comment counts often indicate that your content is resonating with your audience and sparking conversation.

- **Shares**: How many times your Reel has been shared. This is a powerful metric, as it shows that people found your content valuable enough to pass along to others.

- **Saves**: The number of times your Reel has been bookmarked. This is another strong indicator of high-quality, valuable content.

- **Accounts Reached**: The number of unique accounts that have seen your Reel. This metric gives you an idea of your content’s reach beyond just your followers.

- **Engagement Rate**: While Instagram doesn’t provide this metric directly, you can calculate it yourself by dividing the total engagements (likes + comments + shares + saves) by the number of plays, then multiplying by 100.

### 4. Analyze Your Top-Performing Reels

Now that you understand the key metrics, it’s time to look at your best-performing Reels. In the Reels Insights section, you can sort your content by various metrics to see which ones are performing best.

**Pay attention to patterns in your top Reels:**

- What topics or themes seem to resonate most with your audience?

- Are there certain types of content (e.g., tutorials, behind-the-scenes, humor) that consistently perform well?

- What length of Reels tends to get the most engagement?

- Do Reels with certain audio tracks or effects tend to perform better?

Understanding these patterns can help you refine your content strategy and create more of what your audience loves.

### 5. Compare Performance Over Time

To get a true sense of your Reels performance, it’s important to look at trends over time. Instagram allows you to view insights for different time periods, from 7 days up to 90 days.

**To change the time period:**

- In the Insights section, tap the dropdown menu at the top

- Select your desired time frame

Look at how your engagement metrics have changed over time. Are you seeing improvement? Are there certain periods where your engagement spikes or dips? This information can help you understand the impact of different strategies or external factors on your Reels performance.

### 6. Use Third-Party Analytics Tools

While Instagram’s built-in insights are valuable, they have limitations. For more in-depth analysis, consider using third-party analytics tools. These can offer additional metrics, more detailed reports, and often allow you to compare your performance against competitors or industry benchmarks.

Some popular tools include:

- Hootsuite

- Sprout Social

- Later

- Iconosquare

These tools can provide a more comprehensive view of your Instagram performance, including Reels, and often offer features like scheduling and content planning to help streamline your social media management.

### 7. Track Audience Growth and Retention

While engagement metrics are crucial, it’s also important to look at how your Reels are impacting your overall Instagram presence. Pay attention to metrics like:

- **Follower growth**: Are your Reels helping you attract new followers?

- **Profile visits**: How many people are visiting your profile after watching your Reels?

- **Website clicks**: If you have a link in your bio, are your Reels driving traffic to your website?

These metrics can help you understand the broader impact of your Reels strategy on your Instagram presence and business goals.

### 8. Gather Qualitative Feedback

Numbers don’t tell the whole story. To truly understand your Reels’ engagement, pay attention to the qualitative feedback you’re receiving:

- **Read comments**: What are people saying about your Reels? Are they asking questions, sharing their own experiences, or providing feedback?

- **Monitor direct messages**: Are you receiving DMs in response to your Reels?

- **Look at shares**: When people share your Reels, what context are they adding?

This qualitative feedback can provide valuable insights that numbers alone can’t capture, helping you understand the emotional impact of your content and what resonates most with your audience.

## Conclusion

Checking engagement on Instagram Reels is an ongoing process that requires attention to both quantitative metrics and qualitative feedback. By regularly analyzing your Reels performance, you can gain valuable insights into what works for your audience, refine your content strategy, and ultimately create more engaging and effective Reels.

Remember, engagement isn’t just about numbers – it’s about building meaningful connections with your audience. Use these insights to create content that truly resonates with your followers, sparks conversations, and helps you achieve your Instagram goals.

As you continue to experiment with different types of Reels, always keep your audience in mind. What value are you providing them? How can you create content that not only entertains but also informs, inspires, or solves a problem for your viewers? By focusing on creating high-quality, valuable content and using these engagement metrics to guide your strategy, you’ll be well on your way to Reels success.
