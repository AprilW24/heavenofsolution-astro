## Steps to Create Your Miniature Figurine in Gemini AI

- Open your browser and go to gemini.google.com or launch the Gemini app on your phone.

- Look for the **Tools** icon and tap it, then select **Create image**.

- Click plus icon (**+**) and select **Upload files**, then select the photo you want to transform into a miniature (make sure it’s a clear photo where the person is visible).

- Paste this prompt into the text box, “Using the model, create a 1/7 scale commercialized figurine of the characters in the picture, in a realistic style, in a real environment. The figurine is placed on a computer desk. The figurine has a round transparent acrylic base, with no text on the base. The content on the computer screen is the Zbrush modeling process of this figurine. Next to the computer screen is a BANDAI-style toy packaging box printed with the original artwork. The packaging features two-dimensional flat illustrations.”

- Hit the send button (usually an arrow icon pointing right) and wait a few seconds while Gemini processes your request.

- Once the image appears, check if you’re happy with the result or if you want to refine it with additional prompts like “make the figurine sharper” or “improve the lighting.”

- Download your miniature image by clicking the download icon in the top right corner of the generated photo.

- Save it to your device and share it on social media to join the viral trend 📸.

## Tips for Getting Better Miniature Results

The quality of your original photo matters way more than you might think. I learned this the hard way after trying to use blurry selfies and getting mediocre results. For the best miniature figurines, start with a well-lit photo where the person’s face is clearly visible and not too dark or overexposed. Natural lighting works great, and make sure the subject isn’t too far from the camera. Close-up or medium shots tend to work better than full-body photos taken from far away, though both can work if the image is sharp enough.

You can totally customize the prompt to fit your vision. The default prompt creates that classic desk scene with the computer screen and packaging box, which is awesome, but don’t be afraid to experiment. Try changing “computer desk” to “art studio” or “personal workspace” to create different vibes. You can also specify things like “dramatic lighting” or “cinematic style” to change the mood. I’ve had success asking Gemini to place the figurine in creative settings like “surrounded by noodles” or “in a fantasy landscape” for more playful, surreal results 🎨.

If your first attempt isn’t perfect, keep refining. Gemini lets you chat with it, so after the first image generates, you can type follow-up instructions like “make the base more transparent,” “sharpen the details on the face,” or “remove the packaging box.” Sometimes it takes two or three tries to nail exactly what you want, and that’s totally normal. I usually generate a few variations and pick the one that looks the cleanest and most realistic.

## FAQs

### Is Gemini AI free to use for making miniature photos?

Yes, Google Gemini is completely free to use. You just need a Google account to access it at gemini.google.com or through the mobile app, and you can generate as many miniature figurine images as you want without paying anything.

### Can I use group photos or does it only work with single people?

You can absolutely use group photos. The AI will create miniature figurines of all the people in your photo. Just make sure everyone’s faces are clearly visible for the best results, and the prompt will adapt to create multiple figures in the scene.

### How long does it take to generate a miniature photo?

Super quick, usually between 10 to 30 seconds depending on server load. I’ve noticed it’s almost instant most of the time, which is wild considering how detailed the final images are. Way faster than any manual 3D modeling would ever be 🚀.

### Can I turn the miniature images into videos too?

Yes, you can take it further by using video generation tools. Some people use Gemini’s Veo feature or other apps like Flow to animate their miniature figurines. You’d generate the image first in Gemini, then upload it to a video tool with a prompt to add movement or create a rotating display effect.

### What if the AI doesn’t capture my likeness accurately?

Try using a different photo with better lighting and a clearer view of your face. The AI works best with high-quality source images. You can also add refinement prompts after the first generation like “make the facial features more accurate to the reference photo” or “adjust the face to match the original image better” to improve the likeness.
