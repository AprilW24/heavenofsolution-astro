If you use multiple Google accounts, you may want to switch between them in the Google Photos app on your phone. Whether you want to view photos from a different account or upload new images to a specific account, changing your Google Photos account is a straightforward process. In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps to change your Google Photos account on your Android or iOS device.

### Why Change Your Google Photos Account?

There are several reasons why you might want to change your Google Photos account on your phone:

- You have multiple Google accounts for personal and work use and want to keep your photos separate

- You want to view or manage photos from a shared family account

- You’re helping someone else with their phone and need to access their Google Photos account

- You got a new phone and want to use a different Google account than your previous device

No matter the reason, switching Google Photos accounts only takes a few taps. Let’s go through the process step-by-step.

### Steps to Change Your Google Photos Account

**On Android:**

- Open the Google Photos app on your Android phone or tablet

- Tap your profile icon or picture in the top right corner

- You’ll see a list of your Google accounts. Tap the account you want to switch to.

- If the account you want isn’t listed, tap **Add another account**

- Enter the email address and password for the Google account you want to add

- Tap **Sign in**. You should now be signed into the new Google Photos account.

**On iPhone or iPad:**

- Open the Google Photos app on your iOS device

- Tap your profile icon or picture in the top right corner

- Tap **Manage accounts on this device**

- Tap **Add account**

- Choose **Google** as the account type

- Enter the email address and password for the Google account you want to add

- Tap **Sign in**. The app will switch to the new Google Photos account.

That’s it! You’ve now changed your Google Photos account on your phone. Any photos you view, upload, or edit will be associated with the new account you selected.

### Troubleshooting Tips

If you run into any issues while trying to change your Google Photos account, here are a few things to check:

- **Make sure you have a stable internet connection.** Switching accounts requires communicating with Google’s servers, so you’ll need a reliable Wi-Fi or cellular data connection.

- **Check that you’re entering the correct email and password.** If you can’t sign in, double check that you’re using the right account credentials. You may need to reset your password if you’ve forgotten it.

- **Make sure the account has access to Google Photos.** In rare cases, an administrator may have restricted access to Google Photos for the account you’re trying to use. Contact your IT department or the account owner if you’re unsure.

- **Check for app updates.** If you’re running an outdated version of the Google Photos app, you may encounter bugs or glitches. Check the Play Store or App Store for any available updates and install them.

### Conclusion

Changing your Google Photos account on your phone is a quick and easy process that allows you to manage your photos across multiple accounts. Whether you use Google Photos for personal or work reasons, being able to switch accounts in just a few taps is a convenient feature.

By following the steps outlined in this article, you’ll be able to change your Google Photos account on your Android or iOS device in no time. And if you do run into any snags along the way, the troubleshooting tips should help you get back on track.

Now that you know how to change accounts, you can take full advantage of Google Photos’ features, no matter which account you’re using. Happy photo organizing!
