LINE is a widely used messaging app that allows users to communicate, share media, and even make voice and video calls. However, like most social platforms, LINE also provides users with the ability to block others for various reasons, such as privacy concerns or to avoid unwanted interactions.

Being blocked on LINE can be a puzzling experience, as the app doesn’t explicitly notify you when someone has blocked you. However, by paying attention to certain signs and using a few simple methods, you can determine whether you’ve been blocked.

## Steps to Know If Your LINE Is Blocked

### 1. Check Their Profile

One of the first things to do when you suspect someone has blocked you on LINE is to check their profile. If you’re unable to view their profile picture, status updates, or posts, it’s a strong indication that you may have been blocked.

To check a contact’s profile:

- Open your LINE app and go to your friend list

- Tap on the username of the person you think may have blocked you

- If their profile page appears empty or you can’t see their posts and photos, it’s likely that you’ve been blocked

### 2. Send a Message

Another way to determine if you’ve been blocked is by sending a message to the person in question. If you’ve been blocked, your messages will appear to send normally, but the recipient will never receive them, and you won’t see a **Read** status.

To test this:

- Open a chat with the person you suspect has blocked you

- Send a message

- If the message remains unread for an unusually long time, it could mean you’ve been blocked

### 3. Create a Group Chat

Creating a group chat and attempting to add the person who may have blocked you is another effective method to confirm your suspicions.

Here’s how:

- Start a new group chat on LINE

- Try adding the person you think has blocked you to the group

- If you receive an error message or the person doesn’t get added to the group, it’s a sign that you’ve been blocked

### 4. Send a Sticker or Theme

LINE allows users to send stickers and themes as gifts to their contacts. Attempting to send a sticker or theme to someone who has blocked you will result in an error message.

To try this:

- Go to the LINE sticker or theme shop

- Select a sticker or theme and choose the **Send as Gift** option

- Select the person you suspect has blocked you as the recipient

- If you receive an error message stating that you cannot send the gift to this user, it’s likely that you’ve been blocked

## Conclusion

Being blocked on LINE can be a disheartening experience, but by using the methods outlined in this article, you can gain clarity on whether someone has indeed blocked you. Remember that if someone has chosen to block you, it’s best to respect their decision and give them space.

If you find that you have been blocked, don’t take it too personally. Focus on maintaining positive relationships with your other contacts and continue to enjoy the many features LINE has to offer.
