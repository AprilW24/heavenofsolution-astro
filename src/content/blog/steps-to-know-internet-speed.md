Having a fast and reliable internet connection on your phone is important these days. However, sometimes you may experience slow speeds or buffering issues which can be frustrating. The good news is that you can easily check your mobile internet speeds for free using some simple methods. Here are the steps:

#### 1. Use Speedtest App

One of the most popular speed testing services is Ookla’s Speedtest app. You can download their free app on [Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.zwanoo.android.speedtest) or [iOS](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/speedtest-by-ookla/id300704847). Open the app and tap **Go** to start the test. It will measure ping, download speeds, and upload speeds from the nearest server.

#### 2. Try Fast.com

Fast.com is a free web-based speed testing site owned by Netflix. Simply go to [fast.com](https://fast.com/) in your mobile browser and the test will start automatically. It measures your download speed in real-time and updates about every second.

#### 3. Use Google’s Speed Test

Open the Google app on your Android or iOS device and type **speed test**. Tap the blue button to start Google’s built-in speed test. It will measure ping, download and upload speeds.

#### 4. Check Network Stats

On Android 8 or newer, go to **Settings** > **Network & Internet** > **Advanced** > **Network stats**. Here you can view real-time download and upload speeds as you use apps and browse the web.

#### 5. Try Other Speed Testing Sites

There are many other free online speed testing sites you can try on your mobile browser like [SpeedOf.Me](https://www.speedof.me/), [SpeedTest.net](https://www.speedtest.net/), and [TestMy.net](https://testmy.net/).

### Conclusion

Checking your mobile internet speeds regularly allows you to troubleshoot problems, verify you are getting the speeds advertised by your cell provider, and determine when it’s time to upgrade your data plan. Thankfully there are many free apps and online tools that make it quick and easy to test on both Android and iOS devices. For accurate results, make sure to disable Wi-Fi and test over your mobile data connection.
