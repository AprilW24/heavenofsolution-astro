## Mobile Apps Show Easy Ways to Spot Duplicate Text

There are lots of apps for *plagiat* detection on mobile. Some people use Grammarly, QuillBot, Viper, CopyLeaks, or even Skandy, and most of these are easy to use. You just paste your writing, or upload the file, and the app checks billions of sources. The coolest part is, the result arrives fast, marking the copied bits clearly so I don’t have to guess.

These apps have smart features. I can scan in multiple file formats, check in different languages, and get a report that highlights not only *plagiat* but also AI-generated phrases. This helps me see if I’ve missed anything from the web or if AI content sneaks in. A quick check gives me peace of mind before publishing or submitting anything.

## Step-by-Step to Check Plagiarism with AI from Your Phone

1. Open your chosen app for *plagiat* detection.

2. Create a free account or log in if needed.

3. Tap “**Upload**” or “**Paste**” to add your document or text.

4. Select the language if using *multilingual* content.

5. Tap the “**Check Plagiarism**” button.

6. Wait a few seconds for the scan to finish.

7. Review the highlighted sections that match other sources.

8. Download or save the report, if the app allows that.

9. Edit your text to fix any issues flagged as *plagiat*.

10. Scan again to see if you’re all set.

## Use This Prompt to Check for Plagiarism with AI

Copy and paste this prompt when using ChatGPT or most AI chatbots for *plagiat* checking:

“*Please review the text I’ve provided and let me know if any parts match published sources or show signs of plagiat. List all sentences with matches, give the links if possible, and show a percentage of unique text.*“
