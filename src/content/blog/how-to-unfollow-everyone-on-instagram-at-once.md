Instagram has become an integral part of our social media lives, allowing us to connect with friends, family, and influencers from around the world. However, as our following list grows, we may find ourselves overwhelmed by the sheer volume of content in our feed. If you’re looking to declutter your Instagram experience or start fresh, unfollowing everyone at once can be a tempting option. In this article, we’ll explore the steps to unfollow everyone on Instagram using an Android device, along with some important considerations and alternative methods.

## Understanding Instagram’s Unfollow Limitations

Before we dive into the process, it’s crucial to understand that Instagram has certain restrictions in place to prevent spam and maintain the platform’s integrity. The app limits the number of actions you can take within a specific timeframe, including unfollowing users. While the exact limit may vary, it’s generally believed that Instagram allows users to unfollow around 200 accounts per hour. Exceeding this limit could result in temporary restrictions on your account or even a shadowban.

## Manual Method: Unfollowing Users One by One

The safest and most straightforward method to unfollow users on Instagram is to do it manually. While this approach can be time-consuming, it ensures that you stay within Instagram’s guidelines and avoid any potential account issues. Here’s how to unfollow users manually on your Android device:

- Open the Instagram app on your Android smartphone.

- Tap on your profile icon in the bottom right corner.

- At the top of your profile, you’ll see your following count. Tap on it to view the list of accounts you’re following.

- Scroll through the list and tap the “Following” button next to each account you want to unfollow.

- Confirm your action when prompted.

Remember to pace yourself and avoid unfollowing too many accounts in quick succession. It’s recommended to spread out your unfollowing activity over several days or weeks to stay within Instagram’s limits.

## Using Third-Party Apps: A Faster but Riskier Approach

For those looking to unfollow everyone on Instagram more quickly, third-party apps can be an appealing option. However, it’s important to note that using such apps comes with risks and may violate Instagram’s terms of service. If you decide to proceed with this method, do so at your own risk and be aware that it could potentially lead to account restrictions or bans.

One popular app for mass unfollowing on Android is **Unfollow Users**. Here’s how to use it:

- Download and install the Unfollow Users app from the Google Play Store.

- Open the app and sign in with your Instagram credentials.

- Once logged in, tap on the “Unfollow” button.

- The app will automatically start unfollowing accounts from your list.

While this method is faster than manual unfollowing, it’s crucial to exercise caution. Set limits within the app to unfollow only a certain number of accounts per hour, and avoid using it for extended periods.

## Alternative Approach: Unfollowing Least Interacted Accounts

If you’re not ready to unfollow everyone but still want to clean up your feed, Instagram offers a built-in feature to help you identify accounts you interact with least. This can be a great middle ground for decluttering your following list without starting from scratch. Here’s how to use this feature:

- Open Instagram and go to your profile.

- Tap on “Following” to view your list of followed accounts.

- At the top, you’ll see a “Categories” section. Tap on “Least Interacted With.”

- Instagram will show you a list of accounts you’ve had the least interaction with over the past 90 days.

- You can then go through this list and unfollow accounts as desired.

This method allows you to make more informed decisions about which accounts to unfollow based on your actual engagement with their content.

## Best Practices for Mass Unfollowing

Whether you choose to unfollow manually or use a third-party app, here are some best practices to keep in mind:

- **Pace yourself**: Don’t try to unfollow everyone in one sitting. Spread out your unfollowing activity over several days or weeks.

- **Be mindful of Instagram’s limits**: Try to stay under 200 unfollows per hour to avoid triggering Instagram’s anti-spam measures.

- **Consider your connections**: Before unfollowing, think about the potential impact on your personal or professional relationships.

- **Use the “Least Interacted With” feature**: This can help you make more informed decisions about which accounts to unfollow.

- **Take breaks**: If you’re manually unfollowing, take regular breaks to avoid fatigue and maintain accuracy in your choices.

## FAQ

### Is it possible to unfollow everyone on Instagram with one click?

Unfortunately, Instagram doesn’t provide a native feature to unfollow all accounts with a single click. You’ll need to either unfollow manually or use third-party apps, which come with their own risks.

### Will Instagram ban my account if I unfollow too many people at once?

While Instagram is unlikely to ban your account outright, unfollowing too many people too quickly can result in temporary restrictions on your account activities. It’s best to pace yourself and stay within the recommended limit of about 200 unfollows per hour.

### Are third-party unfollow apps safe to use?

Third-party unfollow apps can be risky as they may violate Instagram’s terms of service. Using them could potentially lead to account restrictions or bans. If you choose to use such apps, do so at your own risk and with caution.

### How long does it take to unfollow everyone manually?

The time it takes to unfollow everyone manually depends on how many accounts you’re following and how quickly you work. For a large following list, it could take several days or even weeks if you’re staying within Instagram’s hourly limits.

### Can I recover accounts I’ve unfollowed if I change my mind?

Yes, you can always follow accounts again if you change your mind. However, there’s no automatic way to recover all unfollowed accounts at once. You’ll need to search for and follow each account individually.
