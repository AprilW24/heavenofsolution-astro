If you’ve recently switched from an Android phone to an iPad, one of the first things you’ll want to do is transfer your precious photos. While it’s easy to transfer photos using a computer as an intermediary, you may be wondering how to directly move pictures from your Android device to your new iPad. Luckily, there are several simple methods to wirelessly transfer those memories without needing a PC or Mac.

In this article, we’ll walk through step-by-step instructions for three easy ways to send photos from Android to iPad:

- Using Apple’s Move to iOS app

- Via cloud storage services like Google Photos

- With third-party transfer apps

Before getting started, there are a few things to keep in mind:

- **Ensure both devices are well charged** or plugged in, as transferring a lot of photos can take some time and drain the battery.

- Check that your iPad has **enough free storage space** to accommodate the photos you want to transfer.

- Close any **unnecessary apps** on both devices to avoid interference with the transfer process.

- The methods below work best with **common photo formats** like JPEG and PNG. Some Android devices may store photos in other formats which could have compatibility issues.

With those considerations out of the way, let’s dive into the transfer methods!

### Method 1: Using Apple’s Move to iOS App

Apple provides an official app called **Move to iOS** specifically for migrating data from Android to a new iOS device. It can wirelessly transfer photos, messages, contacts, and more. The catch is that it only works during the initial setup process of a new or factory reset iOS device. If you already finished setup, you’ll need to erase your iPad and start over to use this method.

Here’s how to transfer photos with Move to iOS:

- On your iPad, begin the setup process until you reach the **Apps & Data** screen.

- Select the **Move Data from Android** option.

- On your Android phone, **install the Move to iOS app** from the Google Play Store and open it.

- Tap **Continue** on both devices and agree to the terms and conditions on the Android phone.

- On the Android device, enter the **12-digit code** displayed on the iPad.

- Select **Photos** on the Transfer Data screen and tap Next to begin transferring photos to your iPad.

### Method 2: Via Cloud Storage Services

Another easy option is to use a **cloud storage service** like Google Photos to upload pictures from your Android phone and then access them on your iPad. Google provides 15GB of free storage.

Follow these steps:

- On your Android phone, **download the Google Photos app** if you don’t already have it.

- In Google Photos, tap your **profile picture** and select **Turn on backup**.

- Wait for your photos to finish uploading to Google Photos.

- On your iPad, **install Google Photos** from the App Store and sign in with the same Google account.

- Now you can **access all your backed up photos** on your iPad.

This method also works with other popular cloud services like Dropbox and OneDrive. The main drawback is that transferring a large photo library can take a long time and eat up a lot of data if you’re backing up over a cellular connection.

### Method 3: With Third-Party Transfer Apps

There are also a number of **third-party apps** that specialize in cross-platform file transfers, including photo transfers between Android and iOS. One of the most popular is **Send Anywhere**. It uses Wi-Fi Direct to send photos between devices without going through the cloud.

Here’s how it works:

- Download the **Send Anywhere app** on both your Android and iOS devices.

- Open the app on your Android phone and tap the **+** button.

- Select the **photos** you want to transfer and tap **Done**.

- You’ll see a **6-digit code**. Leave this screen open on your Android phone.

- Open Send Anywhere on your iPad and tap **Receive**.

- **Enter the 6-digit code** from your Android phone.

- The selected photos will be sent to your iPad. You’ll find them saved in the Photos app.

Other third-party apps that work similarly include Feem and Photo Transfer App. Just be aware that some free versions include ads or file size limitations.

## Conclusion

As you can see, it’s quite simple to transfer photos from Android to iPad without using a computer as a go-between. Whether you use Apple’s own Move to iOS service, back up to the cloud with Google Photos, or utilize a third-party transfer app like Send Anywhere, you have several good options to fit your needs and preferences.

The key is to make sure you have enough storage space on your iPad, your devices are fully charged, and you’re on a stable Wi-Fi connection for larger transfers. With these easy wireless methods, you’ll be enjoying your photo memories on that beautiful iPad screen in no time!
