The Google Play Store is the main hub for downloading apps, games, movies, books and more on your Android device. To help keep your account secure, it’s important to use a strong password and change it periodically. If you want to update your Play Store password on your Android phone or tablet, follow these simple steps.

### Why Change Your Play Store Password?

There are a few key reasons to change your Google Play Store password every once in a while:

- **Security**, Using the same password for a long time makes your account more vulnerable if that password is ever leaked or stolen. Changing it regularly helps protect your account.

- **Forgot password**, If you forgot your current Play Store password, you’ll need to reset it in order to regain access to your account and make purchases.

- **Suspicious activity**, If you notice any suspicious activity or unauthorized purchases on your Play Store account, changing your password immediately can help prevent further unauthorized access.

Changing your password only takes a minute and provides valuable security and peace of mind for your Play Store account. Here’s how to do it.

### Step 1: Open Google Play Store

First, open the Google Play Store app on your Android device. You can find the Play Store icon in your app drawer or on your home screen.

### Step 2: Open Settings

In the Play Store app, tap the three horizontal lines icon in the top-left corner to open the menu. Then, select **Settings** from the list of options.

### Step 3: Go to Google Account Settings

Under the **User Controls** section in Settings, tap on **Google account settings**. This will open your main Google account page.

### Step 4: Select Security

On your Google account page, select the **Security** option. Here you’ll see different settings for securing your overall Google account.

### Step 5: Choose Password

Under the **How you sign in to Google** section, select **Password**. You may need to sign in again at this point to verify it’s you making this change.

### Step 6: Enter New Password

Now you’ll see fields to enter your new password. Type in the new password you want to use. Make sure it’s strong, with a mix of upper and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. The new password must be different from your previous password.

### Step 7: Confirm and Save

After typing your new password, you’ll need to enter it a second time to confirm. Then, tap the **Change Password** button to save the new password. That’s it! Your Play Store password is now updated.

### Step 8: Update Password on Other Devices

If you have multiple Android devices that use the same Google account, make sure to update the Play Store password on those other devices as well. You’ll be prompted to sign in again with the new password.

## Conclusion

Changing your Google Play Store password on Android is a quick and easy process that’s important for maintaining the security of your account. By regularly updating your password, you can help prevent unauthorized access to your Play Store account and avoid potential issues like suspicious purchases.

Remember, when creating a new password, always use a strong combination of characters to make it harder to guess. And if you ever suspect that someone else may have gained access to your account, change your password immediately.

By following the simple steps outlined above, you can keep your Play Store account password fresh and your app downloads secure. It only takes a minute to change, but provides valuable protection and peace of mind. Make a habit of regularly updating your Play Store password as part of your overall mobile security hygiene.
