Have you ever accidentally saved the wrong username on the Gmail login screen in Google Chrome on your Android phone? Maybe you mistyped your email address or want to remove an old account you no longer use. Having an incorrect or outdated username automatically populate can be frustrating. Luckily, it’s easy to remove unwanted usernames from the login page.

In this article, we’ll walk through the simple steps to delete a single username from the Gmail sign-in screen on an Android device using the Chrome browser. We’ll also cover how to remove your entire Google account from your phone if needed.

### Why Saved Usernames Appear

First, it’s helpful to understand why incorrect or old usernames get saved in the first place. When you sign into Gmail or another Google service in Chrome, the browser gives you the option to save the username for faster logins in the future. Chrome stores this info, even if you accidentally entered the wrong username.

Chrome’s auto-fill feature is convenient when signing into your regular accounts. But it can cause issues if an incorrect username gets saved or you no longer want quick access to an old account. The login screen will display the saved username as an auto-fill option every time you go to sign in.

### 1. Remove a Single Username

Removing a single saved username from the Gmail login is a quick process:

1. Open the **Chrome app** on your Android phone or tablet

2. Navigate to the **Gmail sign-in page**

3. On the login screen, tap the **username field**

4. Chrome will display a drop-down list of saved usernames

5. Tap and **hold the username** you want to delete

6. Tap **Remove** from the pop-up menu

That’s it! Chrome will no longer auto-fill that username on the Gmail login page. You’ll need to repeat these steps for each username you want to remove.

Keep in mind this only deletes the username from Chrome’s saved data on that specific device. If you’ve saved the same info in Chrome on other devices, you’ll need to remove it separately on each one.

### 2. Removing Other Saved Form Data

The steps above work for any incorrect info saved in Chrome’s auto-fill, not just on the Gmail login page. You can use the same process to delete:

- Mistyped usernames or passwords on other websites

- Outdated shipping or billing addresses

- Incorrect credit card numbers

Just tap and hold on the incorrect form field data wherever it appears and select **Remove** from the menu. It’s an easy way to clean up Chrome’s auto-fill data.

### 3. Delete Saved Passwords

Saved passwords are handled a bit differently than usernames and other form data. To view and delete saved passwords in Chrome on Android:

1. Open the **Chrome app**

2. Tap the **three-dot menu** in the top right corner

3. Go to **Settings > Passwords**

4. Find the **password entry** you want to remove

5. Tap the **trash can icon** to delete it

Be careful when removing passwords, as you’ll have to manually re-enter them the next time you sign into your account. Only delete passwords you’re sure you no longer need.

### 4. Remove a Google Account from Android

In some cases, you may want to completely remove a Google account from your Android device. This signs you out of Gmail, Google Play, and other Google services on your phone.

Reasons you may want to remove an account include:

- You no longer use the account

- You’re switching to a new phone

- You want to troubleshoot account sync issues

Here’s how to remove your Google account on Android:

1. Open your phone’s **Settings** app

2. Tap **Accounts** (or Users & Accounts)

3. Tap **Google**

4. Select the **account** you want to remove

5. Tap **More > Remove account**

After removing the account, you can still use the Gmail app and other Google services by signing in again. But the account will no longer automatically sync data like contacts, calendars, and documents to your phone.

Before removing an account, double-check that you don’t need access to any data stored there, as it will be removed from your device. You may want to back up your Android data first.

### 5. Removing an Account Remotely

What if you’ve lost your phone or let someone borrow it and forgot to remove your account? You can remove account access remotely from a computer:

1. Go to [Google’s security page](https://myaccount.google.com/security)

2. Sign into the **account** you want to remove from the phone

3. In the **Your devices** section, select **Manage devices**

4. Choose the **phone** you want to remove the account from

5. Click **Sign out**

This signs your account out of Gmail and other Google apps on that device. It’s a good security precaution if your phone is ever lost or stolen. You can also use the **Find My** **Device** feature to locate, lock, or erase a lost phone.

### 6. Syncing a New Account

After removing an old or unused Google account from your Android phone, you may want to add a new one. Here’s how:

1. Open your phone’s **Settings** app

2. Tap **Accounts > Add account**

3. Select **Google**

4. Choose **Existing** or **Create account**

5. Sign in with your **account credentials**

Once signed in, you can adjust your account’s sync settings to control which data gets stored on your device:

1. In Settings, tap **Accounts > Google**

2. Select your **account**

3. Tap **Account sync**

4. Use the toggles to **turn sync on or off** for each type of data

Syncing keeps your data backed up and accessible across devices. But syncing a lot of data can also slow down your phone and eat up storage space. Adjust the settings to find the right balance for your needs.

## Conclusion

Knowing how to remove saved usernames and accounts from your Android device gives you more control over your data. It helps keep your personal information private and makes sure you can always access the accounts you need.

Remember, deleting a username from the Gmail login screen won’t affect your Google account, it just removes Chrome’s record of that name on your device. You can still sign in with the username by typing it manually. And if you change your mind, you can always save usernames again in Chrome’s settings.

For better security, get in the habit of signing out of your Google accounts on shared devices or when selling or giving away an old phone. Removing accounts remotely is also a smart way to protect your data on a lost or stolen device.

Managing your accounts and synced data is an important part of owning an Android phone. Take a few minutes to review and remove any old usernames or accounts you no longer use to keep your device running smoothly and securely.
