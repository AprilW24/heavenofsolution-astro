Facebook Marketplace is an increasingly popular platform for individuals to buy and sell locally. With over 1 billion monthly active users on Facebook, Marketplace gives sellers access to a huge audience of potential buyers right in their area.

Once an item sells on Marketplace, the listing is automatically marked as sold and removed from public view. But just because sold listings are hidden, doesn’t mean they can’t provide useful information.

As a seller, being able to view your sold Marketplace listings can help you:

- Determine fair asking prices based on what has recently sold

- Identify which items have the highest demand in your area

- See how quickly items tend to sell

- Evaluate if your descriptions and photos are effective

By analyzing your sold listings, you gain key insights to help maximize future sales. Here’s how to find sold items in Facebook Marketplace.

### Steps

Viewing sold Marketplace listings is easy to do on both desktop and mobile.

#### 1. On Desktop

1. Go to [Facebook Marketplace](https://www.facebook.com/marketplace) and click on the **Selling** tab in the left sidebar.

2. In the **Status** filter at the top, choose **Sold**.

3. This will display all your sold listings from the past 30 days.

#### 2. On Mobile

1. Open the Facebook app and tap on **Marketplace** at the bottom.

2. Tap on the account icon in the top-right corner.

3. Select **Your Listings**.

4. Tap on **Status** and choose **Sold**.

Pro Tip: To analyze specifics around an item, tap into each individual sold listing. This will show the exact sale price, date sold, and buyer details.

### Conclusion

Accessing sold listings provides valuable marketplace data to help guide your selling strategy. By regularly checking sold items, you can set optimal prices, identify top-selling categories, and react to local demand signals.

Be sure to analyze details like:

- How long it took to sell each item

- Which photos and descriptions were most effective

- What prices buyers ultimately paid

Consistently monitoring your sold Marketplace data will lead to more sales and happier customers. So next time an item sells, don’t just forget about it — review the listing and learn from it!
