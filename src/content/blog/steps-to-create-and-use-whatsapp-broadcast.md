You’ve probably been in that situation where you need to send the same message to a bunch of people on WhatsApp, and copy-pasting it one by one just feels exhausting. 😩 Maybe it’s a promo update, an event reminder, or a quick announcement. Doing it manually wastes time and honestly, it’s just not how things should work.

That’s where WhatsApp Broadcast Lists come in. It’s a built-in feature that lets you send one message to multiple contacts at once, and the best part is each person gets it as a private message, not a group chat. No one sees who else got it, and replies come straight to you only.

In this post, I’m going to walk you through exactly how to set up a broadcast list, how to use it the right way, and what limits you should know before you start sending. Whether you’re using it for personal use or for a small business, this guide covers everything you need. 📲

## What Makes WhatsApp Broadcast Different from a Regular Group Chat

A lot of people mix these two up, but they work completely differently. In a group chat, everyone can see each other and reply to the whole group. With a broadcast list, each recipient gets your message as if you sent it to them personally, and their reply only goes back to you, not to anyone else on the list.

Think of it like sending a mass email, but through WhatsApp. You send one message, it lands in each person’s individual chat, and no one knows who else received it. This makes it way more personal and less chaotic than dropping the same message in a crowded group. 🙌

The other big difference is control. In a group, anyone can send messages and things can get noisy fast. With a broadcast, only you send messages to the list. It’s a one-way street, which is exactly what you want when sharing updates, announcements, or promotions.

## How to Create a WhatsApp Broadcast List on Android and iOS Step by Step

This is probably the part you came here for, so let’s get into it. The steps are slightly different depending on your phone, but both are quick to do. 👇

**On Android:**

- Open WhatsApp and head to the **Chats** tab.

- Tap the three vertical dots at the top right corner of the screen.

- Select **New broadcast** from the menu.

- Search for or manually select the contacts you want to add to the list.

- Tap the green checkmark button to create the broadcast list.

- Give your list a name by tapping the pencil icon or the three dots and selecting **Change broadcast list name**.

- Type your message in the chat box, just like a normal chat, and hit send.

**On iPhone (iOS):**

- Open WhatsApp and tap **Settings** at the bottom right.

- Tap **Broadcast Lists** near the top.

- Tap **New List** at the bottom of the screen.

- Search for or select the contacts you want to add.

- Tap **Create** to finish setting up the list.

- Start typing your message right away and send it out.

**For WhatsApp Business users:**

- Tap **Chats**, then tap the three dots or the *kebab menu* icon.

- Select **Business Broadcast** or **New Broadcast**.

- Search and select your contacts.

- Tap the checkmark to create the list.

- You can also create a broadcast directly from a label by going to **Labels**, picking the label, then tapping **Message customers**.

## Key Limits and Tips to Know Before Sending Your First Broadcast

Here’s the thing most people don’t realize until after they hit send. A broadcast list only holds up to **256 contacts** at a time. If your audience is bigger than that, you’ll need to split them into multiple lists and send separately. It’s a bit of extra work, but totally doable. 😅

The bigger catch is this: a contact will only receive your broadcast message if they’ve saved your phone number in their address book. If someone hasn’t saved your number, your message just won’t reach them, even if they’re on your list. So if you’re using this for a business, make sure your customers actually have your number saved first. 📒

A few other things worth knowing before you go all in on broadcasts:

- 🚫 Broadcast lists are **not supported** on WhatsApp Web, Mac, or Windows desktop. You can only send them from your phone.

- ✅ You can create **as many broadcast lists as you want**, so no limit on how many lists you have.

- ✏️ You can **edit a list anytime** by opening it, tapping the three dots, going to **Broadcast list info**, and selecting **Edit recipients**.

- 🗑️ To **delete a broadcast list**, open it, tap the three dots, go to **Broadcast list info**, and tap **Delete broadcast list**.

- 💬 Replies from your contacts land in your **regular individual chats**, never visible to others on the list.

## Conclusion

WhatsApp Broadcast Lists are honestly one of the most underrated features in the app. Once you set it up, you can reach hundreds of people with one message, and it still feels personal to each of them. That’s a big deal, especially if you’re running a small business or managing a community. 🎯

Just remember the two big rules: keep your list under 256 contacts, and make sure your contacts have your number saved. Stick to those, and broadcasts will do exactly what you need them to do.

## FAQs

### Does everyone in the broadcast list see each other?

No, they don’t. Each person gets your message as a private individual chat, and no one on the list can see who else received it.

### What happens if a contact hasn’t saved my number?

They simply won’t receive your broadcast message. WhatsApp won’t notify you either, so it’s best to remind your contacts to save your number before you send anything.

### Can I send a broadcast from WhatsApp Web or desktop?

No, you can’t. Broadcast lists are only available on the WhatsApp mobile app for Android and iOS, not on the web or desktop versions.

### How many people can I add to one WhatsApp broadcast list?

You can add up to 256 contacts per broadcast list. If you need to reach more people, just create additional broadcast lists.

### Can I use broadcast lists on both regular WhatsApp and WhatsApp Business?

Yes, both apps support broadcast lists. WhatsApp Business adds a few extra options like broadcasting through labels, which makes it even more useful for business owners.
