Instagram’s privacy settings allow you to control who can see your posts and stories by letting you approve or deny follow requests. When someone sends you a follow request, you have the option to confirm or delete it. But what if you accidentally tap **Delete** or later regret denying someone’s request? No worries – you can still accept their follow request with a few quick steps.

## Step 1: Find the User’s Profile

The first thing you need to do is find the profile of the person whose follow request you denied. If you remember their username, you can simply type it into the search bar at the top of the app. If not, you may need to do a bit of digging through your past interactions or mutual friends to find them.

## Step 2: Send a Follow Request

Once you’re on the user’s profile, you’ll notice that instead of a **Follow** button, you’ll see a **Request** button. This is because when you denied their initial request, you severed the connection between your accounts. To re-establish it, simply tap the **Request** button to send them a new follow request.

**Note:** If you don’t see a **Request** button, it means the user either hasn’t set their account to private, or they’ve blocked you.

## Step 3: Wait for Approval

After you’ve sent the new follow request, the ball is in the other user’s court. They’ll receive a notification about your request, and it’s up to them to approve or deny it. If they approve it, you’ll start following each other and you’ll be able to see each other’s posts and stories.

**Tip:** If the user doesn’t approve your request right away, don’t panic. They may not have seen the notification yet, or they may be taking time to consider it. Avoid sending multiple requests, as this can come across as pushy or desperate.

## What if They Deny Your Request?

If the user chooses to deny your follow request, unfortunately there’s not much you can do. Instagram doesn’t notify you when your request is denied, so you’ll only know if you check back and see that the **Requested** button has changed back to **Request**.

At this point, it’s best to respect the user’s decision and move on. Continuously sending requests will likely only annoy them and hurt your chances of ever being approved.

## Conclusion

Accidentally denying a follow request on Instagram isn’t the end of the world. By finding the user’s profile and sending a new request, you can easily rectify the situation. Just remember to be patient and respect the user’s decision if they choose to deny your request again.

Managing your Instagram privacy settings and follow requests can seem tricky at first, but with a bit of practice, you’ll be a pro in no time. By controlling who can see your posts and engage with your account, you can create a safer, more enjoyable experience on the platform.
