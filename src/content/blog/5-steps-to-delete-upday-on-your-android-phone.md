Are you tired of seeing the Upday news app on your Samsung Android phone? While Upday aims to provide personalized news, many users find the notifications annoying or simply don’t use the app. Unfortunately, since Upday comes pre-installed on many Samsung devices, it’s not as straightforward to remove as other apps. But don’t worry – with a few steps, you can disable Upday and stop the unwanted notifications. In this article, we’ll walk you through how to turn off Upday on your Samsung Galaxy phone.

## Steps to Disable Upday on Android

Follow these simple steps to get rid of the Upday app:

1. Go to your phone’s **Settings** and select **Apps**.

2. Tap the three-dot menu icon in the top right corner.

3. Select **Show system apps**. This will display all the pre-installed apps, including Upday.

4. Scroll down and find **Upday**. Tap on it to open the app settings.

5. Tap **Disable** and confirm by tapping **Disable App** in the pop-up window.

That’s it! Upday is now disabled and you will no longer receive news notifications from the app. If you ever change your mind, you can always enable Upday again from the same Apps settings menu.

**Note:** On some Samsung devices, you may need to first tap **Force Stop** before the **Disable** option becomes available.

## What If I Can’t Disable Upday?

In some cases, the **Disable** option may be greyed out, preventing you from disabling Upday. This is because Upday is a system app that is deeply integrated into the Android operating system on Samsung devices. However, you can still stop the notifications:

1. Long press on your phone’s home screen and swipe right to access the Upday panel.

2. Tap the gear icon to open Upday settings.

3. Toggle off the **Notifications** option.

While this doesn’t completely remove the Upday app, it will at least stop the annoying notifications from bothering you.

## Conclusion

Pre-installed apps like Upday can be frustrating, especially if you don’t use them. By following the steps outlined above, you can easily disable Upday on your Samsung Galaxy phone and declutter your app drawer. Remember, if disabling doesn’t work, you can still turn off Upday notifications from the app’s settings.

Removing bloatware apps is just one way to optimize your Android device. Consider regularly updating your apps, clearing cached data, and uninstalling unused apps to keep your phone running smoothly. With these tips, you can enjoy a more streamlined, distraction-free mobile experience.
