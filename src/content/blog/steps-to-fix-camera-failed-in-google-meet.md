If you’re trying to join a Google Meet video call on your Android phone or tablet, but you get a **Camera failed** error message, don’t worry, you’re not alone. Many Android users have reported this frustrating issue that prevents them from being seen by others in the meeting. Luckily, there are several steps you can take to troubleshoot and resolve the camera problem so you can get back to your video meeting.

### 1. Check If Other Apps Can Access Your Camera

Before trying to fix the camera issue in Meet, first check if your camera works in other apps on your Android device. Open the built-in Camera app or another app that uses the camera like Instagram or Snapchat. If the camera works there, the issue is likely specific to the Meet app. If the camera doesn’t work in any app, there may be a problem with the camera hardware or your Android operating system.

### 2. Make Sure Google Meet Has Camera Permission

One of the most common reasons for the **Camera failed** error is that the Google Meet app doesn’t have permission to access your camera. Here’s how to check and enable camera access for Meet on Android:

1. Open the **Settings** app on your Android device

2. Tap **Apps & notifications**

3. Find and tap the **Google Meet** app in the list

4. Tap **Permissions**

5. Make sure the **Camera** permission is enabled. If it’s off, tap the switch to turn it on.

After granting camera permission, go back to the Meet app and try joining the video call again. The camera should now work.

### 3. Restart Your Android Device

If Google Meet has camera access but you’re still getting the error, try restarting your Android phone or tablet:

1. Press and hold the **Power** button until the power menu appears

2. Tap **Restart**

3. Wait for the device to fully turn off and back on

4. Open the Meet app and rejoin the video meeting to see if the camera works

Restarting can often fix temporary glitches with the camera or the Meet app.

### 4. Clear Cache and Data for Google Meet

Corrupted data in the Google Meet app can sometimes cause the camera to fail. Clearing the app’s cache and storage can resolve this:

1. Go to **Settings > Apps & notifications**

2. Find and tap on **Google Meet**

3. Tap **Storage & cache**

4. Tap **Clear Cache**, then **Clear Storage**

5. Reopen Google Meet and sign in again

This will erase all Meet app data on your device, so you’ll need to sign in again, but it often fixes camera and other issues.

### 5. Update Android and Google Meet

Using an outdated version of Android or the Google Meet app can lead to compatibility issues and bugs. Check for any available updates:

1. Open the **Settings** app

2. Tap **System > System Update**

3. Tap **Check for update** and install any available Android updates

4. Open the **Google Play Store** app

5. Tap **Menu > My apps & games**

6. Find **Google Meet** in the list and tap **Update** if an update is available

Keeping your device and apps updated will help prevent camera issues and improve performance and security.

### 6. Check Your Internet Connection

A poor internet connection can cause the camera to fail in Google Meet. Make sure your Android device has a strong Wi-Fi or cellular data signal:

- Open a web browser and try loading a webpage. If pages are slow to load, your connection may be weak.

- Turn off Wi-Fi and enable cellular data or vice versa to see if switching the connection fixes the camera issue.

- Move closer to your Wi-Fi router or go to an area with better cellular reception.

- Restart your Wi-Fi router if you’re having connection issues.

### 7. Uninstall and Reinstall Google Meet

If none of the above steps fix your camera issue in Meet, try uninstalling and reinstalling the app:

1. Go to **Settings > Apps & notifications > Google Meet**

2. Tap **Uninstall** to remove the app from your Android device

3. Open the **Google Play Store** and search for **Google Meet**

4. Tap **Install** to reinstall the latest version of the app

5. Open Meet and sign in to see if the camera now works

Reinstalling the app will erase all its data and settings, so you’ll need to sign in and set up Meet again.

## Conclusion

The “Camera failed” error in Google Meet on Android can be caused by various factors, from lack of camera permission to outdated software. By following the troubleshooting steps above, you should be able to resolve the issue and get your camera working in Meet again.

If you’ve tried all these steps and your camera still doesn’t work in the Meet app, there may be an issue with your Android device’s hardware. Consider contacting your device manufacturer’s support for further assistance. You can also try using Google Meet on another device like your computer to see if the camera works there.

Hopefully this guide has helped you fix the frustrating camera issue in Google Meet on Android. Being able to participate in video meetings is more important than ever, so it’s crucial to have a working camera.
