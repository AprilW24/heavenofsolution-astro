Creating travel photos with AI is honestly one of the coolest things I’ve tried with Google Gemini recently. 🌍 With just a text prompt, you can generate realistic, cinematic shots of people in some of the most iconic places on Earth. These prompts I put together cover solo girls, men, couples, and group shots, all designed to look like they came straight from a professional travel photographer.

To use them, open Google Gemini at *gemini.google.com* and head to the image generation section. 🖼️ Paste any of the prompts below directly into the chat box and hit generate. Gemini builds each photo from scratch, so no reference picture is needed. You can swap out the destination, outfit color, or lighting type to get the exact result you want.

### 1. Solo Girl Travel Photo Prompt at Santorini Greece During Sunset

*“A young woman standing on a narrow white-washed cobblestone path in Oia, Santorini, Greece, facing away from the camera and looking out at the caldera. She wears a flowing light yellow maxi dress, a wide-brim straw hat, and flat brown sandals. The iconic blue dome churches sit slightly to her right. The sky is deep orange and pink from a late sunset, with warm golden light casting long, soft shadows on her dress and hair. Shot with a 50mm lens, f/1.8 aperture, shallow depth of field, cinematic travel photography, ultra-realistic, hyper-detailed, 4K resolution, golden hour atmosphere.”*

Neon signs with Japanese characters glow bright behind him, reflecting off the wet pavement beneath his feet. His expression is calm and cool, glancing slightly to the right. Shot from a medium distance, 35mm lens, f/2.0, shallow depth of field, cinematic night street photography, moody atmosphere, ultra-realistic, hyper-detailed, 4K resolution.”*

These prompts give you a solid starting point for generating travel photos that look genuinely real. 📸 Feel free to mix and match destinations, outfits, and lighting styles to make each shot your own. The more specific you get with the details in your prompt, the better Gemini’s output will be.
